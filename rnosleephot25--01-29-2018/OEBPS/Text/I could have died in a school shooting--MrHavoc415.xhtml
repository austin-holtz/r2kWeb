I could have died in a school shooting by MrHavoc415



I remember this day like yesterday and although I wasn't there, it feels like I was. It traumatizes me to this day.

Here's some context: This happened when I was 16, Im 20 now. I was that type of kid that didn't really care about my education. I was typically tardy and absent.

It was a Sunday night and I was debating wether or not I wanted to go to school the next day. I ended up coming to this consensus with myself that I would go, it was a Monday and we all hate Mondays but I would go just to get it out of the way. However, that Monday morning, I woke up with a nasty headache and didn't feel like getting out of bed. I told my parents how I felt and they said that I could stay home but I would have to go the next day. So that morning I stayed in bed trying to get some rest. I ended up taking a couple of aspirins to ease that terrible headache.

At about 9:15 I get this text from my friend. We're super close and had about 3 classes together. So anyway, he messages me saying "Bro, you'll never believe what happened. We're in a lockdown right now. Apparently someone was seen with a gun on campus." I replied with a "holy shit, that's insane. Do they know who it is?". He hit me back with "nah, not yet. Everyone here is going crazy tho, they're telling us not to panic but there's a psycho with a gun over here." "Well keep me posted then" I answered. I headed back to bed, but couldn't sleep. Thinking about that situation had me on edge.

I finally passed out and woke up to the vibration of my phone. I read it and my blood ran cold. "Holy shit man some classrooms got shot up and some people are seriously hurt, I haven't heard who specifically yet but fuck, and the guy who did it, holy fuck you wont believe it." I replied with "damn, who did it? Which classes did they shoot?" I remember shaking uncontrollably as I read the messages. "Mr fucking Tucker did it, and holy fuck the h classes were hit, apparently some kids died, Don't you have classes over there? Shit Im losing my mind man".

I was so confused, Mr. Tucker, the teacher that was fired the previous week. I then received the next message "The cops were in a shootout with Tucker, he shot some cops too. He's dead now". Well apparently a couple weekends ago, a student saw Mr. Tucker dealing drugs and took a video of it. The student showed the principal the next week and he was fired. I never had Mr. Tucker but he seemed like a laid back guy and he never appeared to be drugged up. He had a breakdown when he was told he was fired and left angry, cussing out the rest of the staff. I turned on the news to see this incident being the highlight, a helicopter cam capturing the crime scene.

They didn't announce who was injured, who was dead, etc. They evacuated shortly after and the school was closed for several weeks after. I remember two days had past before they announced the victims. As I saw who they were announcing, I started tearing up, I started shaking uncontrollably. I felt so, so sad for the kids and their families. I noticed some of the victims were kids in the same class I had. Geometry, second period. I came to the realization that I could have been there, I could have died that day. If it wasn't for that headache, I could have shared the same fate as my classmates, God rest their souls. To this day, that event makes me appreciate life a little more, it's terrifying.
